Pakistan

Pakistan is a country in South Asia, located at the crossroads of Central Asia and the Middle East. It shares borders with India to the east, Afghanistan and Iran to the west, China to the north, and the Arabian Sea to the south. Covering an area of 881,913 square kilometers (340,509 sq mi), it is the 33rd-largest country in the world. Pakistan is administratively divided into four provinces, two autonomous territories, and one federal territory. The country features diverse geography, including mountain ranges in the north, fertile plains in the east, and arid plateaus and deserts in the south and west. Around 63% of its population lives in rural areas, though major urban centers are rapidly growing. With a population exceeding 245 milli on as of 2025, Pakistan is the fifth most populous country in the world. The capital city \ is Islamabad, while Karachi is the largest city and economic hub.

Culture

Pakistani culture is a rich tapestry shaped by South Asian, Central Asian, Persian, and Islamic influences. Traditional arts include intricate embroidery, truck art, calligraphy, pottery, and carpet weaving. Music and performance arts feature classical forms like qawwali and ghazals, as well as regional folk dances and storytelling. Cultural practices such as Mehndi ceremonies, religious celebrations, poetry recitals, and tea culture are widely observed. Pakistan has a growing film and media industry (Lollywood), and cricket plays a unifying role in popular life. The country recognizes its cultural heritage through institutions and heritage sites, with six locations inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. As a multilingual and multiethnic society, Pakistan’s cultural identity is both diverse and dynamic.
