Italy

Italy is a country in southern Europe, located on the Italian Peninsula and extending into the Mediterranean Sea. It is bordered by France, Switzerland, Austria, and Slovenia to the north and shares maritime borders with several countries across the Adriatic, Ionian, and Tyrrhenian Seas. Italy includes the islands of Sicily and Sardinia, along with many smaller islands, and spans a total area of 301,340 square kilometers (116,350 sq mi). The country is divided into 20 regions, five of which have special autonomous status. Approximately 35% of Italy’s land is mountainous, with the Alps to the north and the Apennines running down the center. Much of its population resides in urban centers and coastal regions. With a population of about 58 million as of 2025, Italy is the third most populous country in the European Union. The capital and largest city is Rome, historically known as the heart of the Roman Empire and now a major global cultural and political center.

Culture

Italian culture combines its Roman, Renaissance, and Catholic heritage with modern European and global trends. Traditional Italian arts include sculpture, fresco painting, mosaic, and intricate textile and leather craftsmanship; Italy has also given the world enduring contributions in opera, classical music, fashion, and design. Social traditions such as the passeggiata (evening stroll), large family gatherings, religious festivals, and regional food customs are deeply rooted in daily life. Italy has an extensive system for preserving cultural assets and is home to the highest number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the world—59 as of 2025, most of which are cultural. Known globally for its art, cuisine, and style, Italy remains a dominant force in shaping Western and world culture.
