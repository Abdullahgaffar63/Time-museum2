Greece

Greece is a southeastern European country located at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa. Bordered to the north by Albania, North Macedonia, Bulgaria, and Turkey, it is surrounded by the Aegean Sea to the east, the Ionian Sea to the west, and the Mediterranean Sea to the south. The Greek mainland, along with the Peloponnese peninsula and an extensive archipelago of around 6,000 islands and islets—of which roughly 227 are inhabited—covers a total area of 131,957 square kilometers (50,949 sq mi). Greece is divided into 13 administrative regions and is traditionally known for its nine geographic regions. Around 80% of its terrain is mountainous, making it one of the most mountainous countries in Europe. Its population, which is over 10 million as of 2025, is largely concentrated in urban centers, especially along the coasts. The capital and largest city is Athens, a cultural and historical hub of global significance

Culture

Greek Popular Culture Contemporary Greek culture blends ancient heritage with influences from Europe, the Middle East, and modern Western trends. Traditional Greek arts include pottery, embroidery, jewelry, wood carving, and icon painting; performance traditions such as ancient theater, Byzantine chant, folk dance, and rebetiko music remain deeply rooted. Cultural practices include the celebration of religious festivals, traditional cuisine, coffeehouse gatherings (kafeneio), shadow puppetry (Karagiozis), and storytelling. Greece also preserves customs like the Greek Orthodox Easter, name days, and village festivals (panigyria). The country has an established framework for preserving its rich cultural legacy, including both tangible and intangible heritage. Nineteen sites in Greece are inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List, most of which are of cultural significance. Often referred to as the cradle of Western civilization, Greece continues to exert a profound influence on global culture.
