Chinese Art Masterpieces

Along the River During the Qingming Festival

This 12th-century painting by Zhang Zeduan depicts bustling city life during the Song Dynasty. Considered one of China's most famous paintings, it showcases the prosperity of Bianjing (modern Kaifeng).

Japanese Art Masterpieces

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai's iconic 1831 woodblock print from his series "Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji." This work has become a global symbol of Japanese art and culture.

Korean Art Masterpieces

Portrait of Kang Hui-an

18th-century portrait of a Confucian scholar by an unknown artist. Represents the yangban (aristocratic) class during the Joseon Dynasty and the importance of Confucian values.

Egyptian Art Masterpieces

Nebamun Hunting in the Marshes

Fragment of a tomb-chapel painting from Thebes (c. 1350 BCE). Shows Nebamun hunting birds in the Nile marshes, symbolizing triumph over chaos and the renewal of life.

Palestinian Art Masterpieces

Church of the Nativity Mosaics

12th-century Crusader-era mosaics in Bethlehem's Church of the Nativity, one of the oldest continuously operating churches. Depicts Christ's ancestors and church councils.

Greek Art Masterpieces

Akrotiri Frescoes

Bronze Age wall paintings from Santorini (c. 1600 BCE). The "Flotilla Fresco" shows a vibrant maritime scene with ships sailing between towns, demonstrating Minoan artistic skill.

Italian (Roman) Art Masterpieces

Venus de Milo

Ancient Greek marble statue (c. 100 BCE) of Aphrodite discovered on Milos. Though Greek, it became emblematic of Roman taste for Hellenistic art and was widely copied.

Pakistani Art Masterpieces

Priest-King of Mohenjo-daro

Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2000 BCE) soapstone sculpture from Mohenjo-daro. Demonstrates the sophisticated craftsmanship of one of the world's earliest urban cultures.
